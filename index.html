Michael (Junghoon) Lee

Ph.D. Student in Accounting, Cornell University

I am a Ph.D. student in Accounting at Cornell University. My research examines how accounting information interacts with corporate investment decisions, particularly under uncertainty and through the lens of real options theory.

Methodologically, I use textual analysis, including NLP and LLM-based approaches, to study firms’ disclosure, information environments, and investment decisions.

Prior to joining Cornell, I worked for four years at PricewaterhouseCoopers in Seoul as an auditor and advisor. As a member of PwC Korea’s in-house valuation specialist group, I worked on valuation engagements involving goodwill, unlisted stocks, convertible bonds, bonds with warrants, and purchase price allocation. I also participated in buy-side due diligence, accounting advisory projects, internal control engagements, and audits across multiple industries.

I am a Certified Public Accountant and have also served as a mentor for new staff CPAs, organizing educational sessions for junior professionals.
